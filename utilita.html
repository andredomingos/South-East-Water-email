HOW WILL THE PRICE CHANGE TAKE EFFECT?
IF YOU HAVE A SMART METER
Next time you top-up, your meter will be updated ready for the change. If your top-ups update automatically there is nothing else you need do.
IF WE CAN'T REMOTELY UPDATE YOUR METER
You will see a longer top-up code than your usual 20/40 digit code. You need to enter this 60 digit 'tariff change' code printed on your receipt into your electricity and/or gas meter, Freedom Unit or In-Home Display. If you do not enter the tariff change code into your meter, any top-ups you make up to the 30 September 2018 will still work as usual, however any top-up you make on or after 1 October 2018 will display an error message on your smart meter as the 'tariff change' top-up must be entered first.
IF YOU HAVE A KEY OR CARD METER
Next time you top-up, your meter will be updated with the price change and there is nothing else you need to do.
IF YOU PAY BY DIRECT DEBIT, CASH OR CHEQUE
Your next bill will reflect the changes, you just need to carry on as usual.

EXTRA INFORMATION
YOUR RIGHT TO CANCEL
If these price changes are not acceptable to you and you decide to change supplier as a result, you'll need to tell us.
Providing we get notice of this from your new supplier within 20 working days and the new supplier takes over within a reasonable period of this date, we will not enforce this tariff change.
Where you wish to transfer to a new supplier as above, but this is delayed as a result of us objecting because of a debt on your account, we will tell you we have objected to the transfer. Providing you settle any outstanding debt within 30 working days of us notifying you and then you leave us within 20 working days, we will not enforce this tariff change. We do not charge termination fees in order for you to end your supply with us.
IF YOU HAVE OUTSTANDING CHARGES
Whilst we always try to provide you with the cheapest prices, it's possible that you may be able to find a cheaper tariff by switching suppliers. Remember - if you have any outstanding debt on your meter(s), we may object to your transfer, so please clear any outstanding balance prior to switching supplier. Should you hold a debt on your prepayment meter up to 500 per meter, there may still be the opportunity to facilitate your switching supplier through the Debt Assignment Protocol. This requires you to consent for your debt information to be shared with your chosen new supplier in order for them to purchase the debt from us and allow the switch to take place.
Please be aware, should you not consent to your debt information being shared, we are unable to proceed through the Debt Assignment Protocol. Similarly, if we do not receive a request to transfer the debt from your new supplier, this may also mean the switch is unachievable. You will be notified by us if your debt has been accepted to proceed through the Debt Assignment Protocol.
